All five of the non-classical truth table described logics we are interested in, are extensions of classical logic. For the logical not ~ reference table, any truth values not used by a given logic are omitted for that logic.

~
~true ↦false
~false ↦true
~unknown ↦unknown
~contradiction ↦contradiction

The ⇔ table entries are computed from the definition of ⇔ . The interpretation of these into English is surprising for both Belnap's four-valued logic, and Franci's, handling of unknown ⇔ contradiction.

Syntactical rules for inverting &

The ├ syntactical inference rules are actually ⟛ syntactical equivalence rules, but this requires the proof by cases inference rule to upgrade.

All
(P & Q ↦ true) ⟛ (P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ true)
Classical
(P & Q ↦ false) ├ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true))
Kleene's strong three-valued logic
(P & Q ↦ false) ├ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown))
(P & Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true))
Kleene's weak three-valued logic
(P & Q ↦ false) ├ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true))
(P & Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false))
Lisp/Prolog
(P & Q ↦ false) ├ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown))
(P & Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false))
Belnap's four-valued logic
(P & Q ↦ false) ├ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown))
(P & Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true))
(P & Q ↦ contradiction) ├ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ unknown))
Franci
(P & Q ↦ false) ├ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown))
(P & Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true))
(P & Q ↦ contradiction) ├ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ unknown))

Syntactical rules for inverting ∨

Again, the ├ syntactical inference rules are actually ⟛ syntactical equivalence rules, but this requires the proof by cases inference rule to upgrade.

The corresponding rules for ⇒ are obtained by cleaning up using the uniform substitution, authorized by the definition of ⇒.

All except Franci
(P ∨ Q ↦ false) ⟛ (P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ false)
Classical
(P ∨ Q ↦ true) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false))
Kleene's strong three-valued logic
(P ∨ Q ↦ true) ├ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown))
(P ∨ Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false))
Kleene's weak three-valued logic
(P ∨ Q ↦ true) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false))
(P ∨ Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown))
Lisp/Prolog
(P ∨ Q ↦ true) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown))
(P ∨ Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true))
Belnap's four-valued logic
(P ∨ Q ↦ true) ├ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ contradiction))
(P ∨ Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false))
(P ∨ Q ↦ contradiction) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ false))
Franci
(P ∨ Q ↦ true) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ true)) ∨ ((P ↦ true) & (Q ↦ false))
(P ∨ Q ↦ false) ├ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ contradiction)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ false))
(P ∨ Q ↦ unknown) ├ ((P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ false) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ unknown)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ false)) ∨ ((P ↦ unknown) & (Q ↦ contradiction))
(P ∨ Q ↦ contradiction) ├ (P ↦ contradiction) & (Q ↦ contradiction)

These are obtained by applying the inference rules of constructive dilemma, absorption, commutativity of ∨, and associativity of ∨. We start by assuming these principles are classical logic statements about the target logic. [This is how we translate truth table formatted calculations, to symbolic logic.]
